The Big Idea

What are we building?

A website where you design a planet — its star, size, atmosphere, water, spin, and orbit. The simulator then uses real physics equations to predict what that world is actually like: its gravity, temperature, radiation, and whether it could ever hold an atmosphere. Finally, an AI layer imagines the alien life that could evolve there — its ecosystems, food chains, and creatures.

🪐

You design

📐

🌱

AI imagines

Layer 1 · The Rigorous Core

The Physics Engine

Every output comes from a real equation you can explain and defend. This is the part that proves you're a physicist.

Solid Physics

⚖️ Surface Gravity

g = g₀ × (Mass ÷ Radius²)

Heavier planet → stronger pull. Bigger radius → weaker pull at the surface. Tells you how much you'd weigh.

Solid Physics

🌡️ Equilibrium Temperature

T = 278 × L^¼ × dist^−½ × (1−albedo)^¼

Balances the starlight coming in against the heat the planet radiates away. Brighter star or closer orbit → hotter.

Solid Physics

🌊 Cosmic Shoreline

escape velocity vs. stellar radiation

The clever bit: does the planet's gravity win against the star stripping its air? Decides "keeps atmosphere" vs. "bare rock."

Refined Estimate

♨️ Greenhouse Boost

surface T = equilibrium T + greenhouse warming

CO₂, water vapour and methane trap heat. Earth would be −18 °C without it! Too much → runaway, Venus-style.

Proxy Index

☢️ Radiation Level

based on star type + distance

Red dwarfs flare violently; closer orbits get blasted. A physically-motivated score, not an exact number.

Scoring

🎯 Habitability Score

liquid water? + gravity? + atmosphere? + stable?

Combines everything into one 0–100 headline number with a clear verdict.

Layer 2 · The Creative Payoff

The Biosphere Generator

Here the physics turns into biology. First your rules engine reasons from the conditions; then AI brings it to life with vivid creatures, food chains, and evolutionary stories.

🧠 Your Rules (the science)

Logic you design, e.g.:
• Too cold for surface water → life goes underground
• Red dwarf star → plants are black/red, not green
• High gravity → short, strong, squat organisms
• No oxygen → chemosynthesis near vents

✨ The AI (the imagination)

Takes your rules' conclusions and writes the world up — naming organisms, sketching a food chain, telling an evolutionary story. The logic is yours; the AI just makes it sing.

🔬 Worked example

Planet: 50% methane, 20% nitrogen, orbiting a red dwarf.

Reasoning: Red dwarf light is dim & red → photosynthesis is possible but uses dark red/black pigments. A methane-rich, oxygen-poor air points to chemosynthesis near geothermal vents. Result: "Expect chemosynthetic microbial mats underground, and dark photosynthetic life clinging to the surface near warmth."

⚠️ Our guiding principle: scientific honesty.

The biology layer is informed speculation, never a prediction. Real scientists know that even "signs of life" like oxygen or methane can be made without life. So our app will always say "these conditions could support X" — never "life detected." Being honest about uncertainty is what makes a project look mature, not less impressive.
